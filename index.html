Double Gold at Milano-Cortina 2026

A Personal Journal of the Olympic Hockey Tournament

Tuesday, Feb 17, 2026

Men’s Qualification

Upon first arriving in Italy I met up with fellow solo traveler, Connor Mohr, to get the lay of the land and make plans to watch some hockey together. We ended up first attending the men’s qualification round between Sweden and Latvia on Tuesday night to scout out the competition Team USA was to face the following night and also get my first live glimpse of Olympic hockey. I was in awe from the start.

Scouting the competition: Sweden vs. Latvia.

We were able to squeeze in a late nightcap after the game meeting up with fellow Chicago hockey player and neighbor Jack Seney along with his fiance. Over a drink, they told me the location of the USA “Winter House”, which was a swanky hotel and bar that became the hospitality headquarters for Team USA in Milan. I promptly saved the location to my Google Maps and forgot all about it, not knowing it would become key intel later on in my trip.

Nightcap with Jack.
Wednesday, Feb 18, 2026

Men’s Quarterfinal

USA vs. Sweden.
The summery Corona cups.

Connor and I had seats together for the 9:15pm quarterfinal matchup of USA vs. Sweden. Watching Team USA come onto the ice in glorious blue against Sweden’s iconic Tre Kroner yellow jersey, I couldn’t help but think of Herb Brooks (ok, Kurt Russell) angrily yelling “you’re playing SWEDEN…IN THE OLYMPICS!” in Miracle. This was my first ever glimpse of the USA hockey team live in person - something I have quite literally been dreaming about for as long as I can remember. I was beyond pumped up.

After a scoreless first period, we took to the concourse. Shockingly, the only beer served at the Olympic events was the summery (and overrated) Corona, but at least they came in cool cups. Naturally, I was wearing my favorite hometown player’s jersey in Milan. A Michigan Wolverine who went on to become captain of the Detroit Red Wings and key player & leader for Team USA, Dylan Larkin. As we got through the beer line after that first intermission, Connor pointed out a big group of people also wearing #21 jerseys and so I absolutely had to go say hello and introduce myself.

The Larkin Family crew.
Rolling with the "Keller Group."

It turned out to be Kevin Larkin (his dad), Colin Larkin (his brother), and a whole crew of family and friends that had traveled to Milan. We had some fun chatting and they couldn’t have been nicer people who I would end up seeing at nearly every intermission of each USA men’s game through the rest of the tournament. I’m sure they were meeting a ton of people, but I think Kevin’s dad thought I was somehow connected to the Clayton Keller group. At first I tried to explain that I’m just a fan, but eventually I thought it best to just roll with it, an attitude that would continue to pay dividends for me on this trip.

Guess who scored the first goal of the game? It had to be Dylan Larkin. Thankfully, the boys got it done and another former Wolverine, Quinn Hughes, buried the game winner. Luck or destiny may have been turning our way, and I allowed myself to dream about the games ahead.

Thursday, Feb 19, 2026

Women’s Gold Medal

The quest for Gold begins.

After having sweated out the late overtime win by the men the prior night, I took my time getting up and moving. This was the day of the women’s gold medal game, and as expected the matchup was USA against Canada. These were the first tickets my dad and I had purchased more than a year prior to the event. I was quite excited to go, and happy to have a fellow fan to use my dad’s ticket in Connor. We decided to meet up for pregame beers around 1pm, and landed on the canal district, or Navigli, as a good spot.

Moments before puck drop. The energy was massive.

Finally a break with a long outlet pass during a bad change and American hero Megan Keller, of Green Valley Estates in Farmington Hills, MI, pulled off the “Star-Spangled Dangle” golden goal to bring home a gold medal in epic fashion. Connor and I were in disbelief at what we just saw and the fact that our hometown hero had pulled that off. Unreal. We watched the anthems and celebrations until the very last player left the ice and were so happy and thankful to be able to witness that moment.

Pure disbelief.
The ceremony.
Team USA: 2026 Olympic Champions.
Friday, Feb 20, 2026

Men’s Semifinal

On the morning of the semifinals, I found a quiet moment to myself, sitting under the ancient pillars of the Porta Ticinese Medievale with a coffee in hand. We ended up finding a spot that served arguably the best Cacio e Pepe in all of Milan. We spent the afternoon lingering over incredible pasta and wine before heading to the rink.

Pregame with Viktor and the Swedes.

As the clock was winding down in the semifinal, I had already decided I was going to attend the gold medal game no matter how expensive it was going to be. Thankfully, I had some intel on a resale app. I found a “partially obstructed” lower bowl seat, which as it turns out, was directly next to the USA women’s team still celebrating their own Gold.

Champions in the seats.
With superfan Jason Kelce.
Closing down the Tutti Fritti.
Sunday, Feb 22, 2026

Men’s Gold Medal

Men's warmups. The stage is set.

To be honest, the game itself is a blur of adrenaline and anxiety. I had an incredible lower-bowl seat, but I was a nervous wreck, watching Hellebuyck put on a literal masterclass in goaltending. When the game went to overtime, I couldn't sit still. I was pacing the concourse, unable to look but unable to turn away. Then it happened: McDavid had a point-blank one-on-one chance that got shut down, the play transitioned the other way, and the rest is hockey history. Bedlam.

History begins.
Pacing the halls.
The flag hanging on the glass as history unfolded.

After the anthem played, the weight of it finally hit me. I stood there crying, kissing my dad’s pins on my USA jersey. It was, without question, the greatest sports moment of my life.

For my dad.
Gold for USA.
Champions.

At the same time, both my mom and my dad’s buddy Tony were sending celebratory messages and pictures showing how they were including the memory of my dad in their celebrations back home.

Celebrations back home.
Dad was there with us.

Suddenly, we were inside the freaking team USA gold medal after party. The first person I saw was Quinn Hughes, then the legend himself, Mike Eruzione.

Quinn Hughes.
The Captain.
Brady Tkachuk.
Colin and Jack.
The Tkachuk family.
McAvoy and Eichel.

It was the perfect, unbelievable capstone to the trip. I know my dad would’ve been loving it all.

In memory of Dad.
