Thursday, February 19, 2026

Women’s Gold Medal

After having sweated out a tight overtime win by the USA men against Sweden in the quarterfinals the prior night, I took my time getting up and moving. This was the day of the women’s gold medal game, and as expected the matchup was USA against Canada. These were the first tickets my dad and I had purchased more than a year prior to the event. I was quite excited to go, and found a fellow fan to use my dad’s ticket in Connor Mohr. We decided to meet up for pregame beers around 1pm, and landed on the canal district, or Navigli, as a good spot.

As we walked along the canal it was striking how many more Canadian fans were out and about than Americans. The first beer-focused place we could find open was the Navigli Craft Beer bar, which turned out to be a great spot to start. Connor knew the bar the Canadians had taken over - the Blues Canal - and we decided to pop in for a little fun. We heard a couple playful comments, but no strong chirps. A reporter approached us, Robyn Doolittle of the Globe & Mail. We told her we were confident and we even thought the Canadian fans were nervous since the USA women had been so dominant.

The "audacity" of wearing full USA gear in a Canadian takeover bar.

The game was intense. Hilary Knight tipped in a shot in front of the net with the goalie pulled to tie the game in the dying minutes. On to OT. Finally, a break with a long outlet pass and American hero Megan Keller, of Farmington Hills, MI, pulled off the “Star-Spangled Dangle” golden goal to bring home a gold medal in epic fashion. Connor and I were in disbelief. We watched the anthems and celebrations until the very last player left the ice.

Friday, February 20, 2026

Men’s Semifinal

On the morning of the semifinals, I found a quiet moment to myself, sitting under the ancient pillars of the Porta Ticinese Medievale with a coffee in hand. I struck up a conversation with a fellow named Davidson. It turned out he was living in California but was originally from Michigan, so we hit it off immediately over the hometown connection. We ended up finding a spot that served arguably the best Cacio e Pepe in all of Milan.

At the shuttle pickup point, we ran into a group of Swedes led by Viktor. They were a riot. After the doubleheader, where USA overmatched Slovakia for a stress-free win, I ducked into a spot near my Airbnb for a nightcap - the Tutti Fritti. To my total surprise, standing right there outside the beer window was the same group of Swedes. We closed down the bar together. I’d run into them one last time that Monday—and right after that, I spotted Mike Eruzione casually strolling through town.

Late night with Viktor and the Swedes at Tutti Fritti.
Sunday, February 22, 2026

Men’s Gold Medal

For the championship game, the nerves were already red-lining. Inside the concourse, I spotted Dylan Larkin’s family; at each intermission, I found his brother Colin for a fist-bump for good luck. The game itself is a blur of adrenaline and anxiety. I had an incredible lower-bowl seat, but I was a nervous wreck, watching Hellebuyck put on a literal masterclass in goaltending.

Then it happened: McDavid had a point-blank one-on-one chance that got shut down, the play transitioned the other way, and the rest is hockey history. Bedlam. I sprinted to the front row as the celebration erupted, throwing my flag toward the ice. It got snagged on the glass, hanging there as this perfect backdrop for the chaos. After the anthem played, the weight of it finally hit me. I stood there crying, kissing my dad’s pins on my USA jersey. It was, without question, the greatest sports moment of my life.

The flag on the glass—the best moment of my life.

Post-game, we met Shawn from Colorado and a country singer from Pittsburgh named Will. Our group swelled to seven fans, including a United pilot and his wife. We shared heavy, beautiful moments celebrating the ones we had lost—Will’s dad, Shawn’s niece, and my own father. As the night progressed, I led the group to the "Winter House" - the Team USA after-party. When the greeter asked if I was "also with Quinn," I didn't blink: "You bet I am."

Suddenly, we were inside the freaking team USA gold medal after party. The first person I saw was Quinn Hughes, then the legend himself, Mike Eruzione. I took a selfie with both of them, as well as Brady Tkachuk and his gold medal. I chatted with Jack Hughes for a second about "Greenie" and he asked if I was a "UMich guy." I spent the rest of the night nursing Michelobs and taking it all in—including Eichel and McAvoy accidentally falling into me during a loving bro moment while I was taking a selfie.

A legend and a new gold medal memory.

I know my dad would’ve been loving it all.
