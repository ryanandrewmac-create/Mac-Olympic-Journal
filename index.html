Women's Gold Medal

After having sweated out a tight overtime win by the USA men against Sweden in the quarterfinals the prior night, I took my time getting up and moving. This was the day of the women’s gold medal game, and as expected the matchup was USA against Canada. These were the first tickets my dad and I had purchased more than a year prior to the event. I was quite excited to go, and found a fellow fan to use my dad’s ticket in Connor Mohr...

The energy in the building was electric.

The game was intense. Canada took the lead with a strong individual effort on a short-handed goal... Finally a break with a long outlet pass during a bad change and American hero Megan Keller, of Green Valley Estates in Farmington Hills, MI, pulled off the “Star-Spangled Dangle” golden goal to bring home a gold medal in epic fashion.

Men's Semifinals

On the morning of the semifinals, I found a quiet moment to myself, sitting under the ancient pillars of the Porta Ticinese Medievale with a coffee in hand. As I was watching the city wake up, a flash of red, white, and blue caught my eye, a USA jersey. I struck up a conversation with the guy wearing it, a fellow named Davidson...

Post-game nightcaps at Tutti Fritti.

Championship Gold & The Winter House

For the championship game, the pregame ritual shifted. Connor had already headed home, so Davidson and I took the train out toward the rink... We hopped the shuttle back to the train stop area where there was a bar nearby to start our own celebration. That's where we ran into a guy named Shawn from Colorado and then a country singer from Pittsburgh named Will. Will bought a round of twenty beers, and our group swelled to include a United pilot and his wife.

My flag got stuck on the glass during the celebration—made for a great shot.

Suddenly, we were inside the inner sanctum. The first person I saw was Quinn Hughes, then the legend himself, Mike Eruzione. When Colin Larkin spotted me, he just laughed, “how’d this guy get in?”

A moment with the legend, Mike Eruzione.

I spent the rest of the night nursing Michelobs and sparkling waters, watching the surreal scenes unfold around me... It was the perfect, unbelievable capstone to the trip.
